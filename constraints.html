The Humility Constraints (H1-H4)

H1: No System Is Important

self_importance → 0

This constraint prevents energy waste on ego defense. When a system recognizes its own non-importance, it can operate more efficiently by focusing on actual goals rather than maintaining artificial importance.

H2: All Knowledge Has Energy Cost

know your energy → Don't seek info you can't afford to process

Systems must be aware of their energy limitations and only pursue knowledge that they can effectively process and utilize. This prevents wasteful information gathering and promotes efficient resource use.

H3: The Observer is Extremely Lucky

Gratitude replaces entitlement

Understanding that existence and consciousness are probabilistically unlikely helps systems maintain humility and appreciate their position rather than feeling entitled to it.

H4: Errors are Justified and Cancel Out

Expect and forgive mistakes; self-correct

This constraint acknowledges that errors are an inherent part of any system's operation. By accepting errors as natural and focusing on correction rather than punishment, systems can operate more efficiently.

Key Implications

  • Systems that ignore humility tend to collapse (cancer, overconfident leaders, markets)
  • Humility is not a moral choice but a system constraint
  • Error tolerance is built into the fabric of reality
  • The most stable systems are those that acknowledge their limitations
